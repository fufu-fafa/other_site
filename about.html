How It Works

  1. The 555 timer continuously switches between high and low states, creating a pulse wave output.
  2. The frequency and duty cycle of the blinking LED are controlled by the 10kΩ and 1kΩ resistors and the 10µF capacitor.
  3. Since Pin 2 (Trigger) and Pin 6 (Threshold) are shorted, the capacitor charges and discharges repeatedly, cycling the timer.
  4. The LED blinks as the output switches between HIGH and LOW.
